Alignment

The design principle of alignment is that everypart of a site is aligned with the neighboring parts and the page as a whole. None of the placement should be by accident. Take advantage of the way that aspects of a page line up and use that alignment to draw viewers to certain parts of a page.

The website YouTube.com is an excellent example of a website that uses alignment to make the page both visually appealing, as well as functional. All of the video content that is displayed on the main page is presented in a grid. The videos that are within the same row are related in some way, and a user is immediately able to tell this because of the videos being presented in a line.
